# Chapter 14: Health Psychology and Introduction to Psychological Disorders

## Summary

This chapter bridges the biology of stress with the classification of mental
illness. The first half covers health psychology: the biopsychosocial model,
eustress vs. distress, adverse childhood experiences, Selye's General
Adaptation Syndrome, fight-flight-freeze and tend-and-befriend responses,
coping strategies, and positive psychology (well-being, resilience, signature
strengths). The second half introduces the clinical classification framework —
DSM and ICD systems, the diathesis-stress model, stigma, and the four
explanatory perspectives on disorders — then surveys anxiety disorders
(specific phobia) and introduces evidence-based psychotherapy and schizophrenia.
