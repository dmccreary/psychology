# Chapter 11: Social Psychology: Attitudes, Attribution, and Influence

## Summary

This chapter examines how other people shape our thoughts, feelings, and
actions at the individual level. Attribution theory explains how we assign
causes to behavior — and why we systematically err via the fundamental
attribution error and actor-observer bias. Students then explore attitude
formation, cognitive dissonance, and persuasion (including the elaboration
likelihood model). Classic compliance and obedience research is introduced
through Asch's conformity studies, and the chapter closes with prosocial
concepts including social norms, social influence theory, social facilitation,
and the special case of stereotype threat.
