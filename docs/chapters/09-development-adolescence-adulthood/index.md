# Chapter 9: Development: Adolescence Through Adulthood

## Summary

This chapter continues developmental coverage from adolescence through late
adulthood. Students examine formal operational thinking, identity formation
(Erikson's identity vs. role confusion), and Kohlberg's stages of moral
development alongside Gilligan's ethics-of-care critique. The chapter then
traces adult cognitive changes — including fluid vs. crystallized intelligence
— through aging, and concludes with social clock pressures, end-of-life
perspectives, and the Kübler-Ross grief stages. Piaget's concrete and formal
operational stages and Vygotsky's ZPD and scaffolding complete the cognitive
development picture.
