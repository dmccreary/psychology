# Chapter 10: Learning and Conditioning

## Summary

This chapter covers how experience modifies behavior through three major
frameworks. Classical conditioning (Pavlov) is traced from acquisition
through extinction, spontaneous recovery, generalization, discrimination,
and conditioned emotional responses including taste aversion. Operant
conditioning (Skinner) covers reinforcement types, punishment, schedules
of reinforcement, shaping, and learned helplessness. Bandura's social
learning theory introduces observational learning and the Bobo doll study.
The chapter also addresses cognitive factors in learning — latent learning,
cognitive maps, self-efficacy, the growth mindset, and neuroplasticity.
