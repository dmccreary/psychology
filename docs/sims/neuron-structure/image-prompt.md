# Image Generation Prompt — Neuron Structure

## Prompt

A detailed biological textbook illustration of a **multipolar motor neuron** in full
landscape orientation, showing the complete neuron from dendrites on the left to
axon terminals on the right, with an inset zooming into the synapse.

**No text, labels, arrows, callout lines, leader lines, numbers, or annotation
marks anywhere in the image.** The image must be entirely free of embedded text.

### Main neuron (left two-thirds of image)

- **Dendrites** — multiple branching, tapering tree-like processes extending to the
  left and upper-left from the soma. Use cyan-blue coloring. Show 4–6 branching
  dendrites of varying length.
- **Cell body (soma)** — large, round or slightly oval, soft pink. Prominently
  centered in the left portion of the image. Clearly the largest structure.
- **Nucleus** — inside the soma: a large round nucleus in violet-purple with a
  visible darker nucleolus at its center.
- **Axon hillock** — a distinct cone-shaped narrowing at the lower-right edge of the
  soma where the axon emerges, slightly more orange-tinted than the soma.
- **Axon** — a long, straight, slender pale yellow process running horizontally to
  the right from the axon hillock. The axon runs roughly through the center of the
  image horizontally.
- **Myelin sheath** — thick cream/ivory segmented wrapping around the axon. Show
  3–4 distinct myelin segments (internodes). Each segment has a slightly rounded
  elliptical profile. The myelin wrapping should show a faint spiral layering to
  suggest multiple Schwann cell membrane wraps.
- **Schwann cell nucleus** — visible at the edge of each myelin segment: a small,
  oval, slightly darker green-tinted nucleus belonging to the Schwann cell, seated
  outside the innermost wrap. Show one Schwann cell nucleus per internodal segment.
- **Nodes of Ranvier** — 2–3 narrow gaps (constrictions) between myelin segments
  where the bare axon is visible. The node should appear as a pinched-off dark ring
  or gap, contrasting clearly with the adjacent myelin segments. Color the exposed
  axon membrane at the node in a slightly warmer pink-red tone to distinguish it.

### Synaptic inset (right one-third of image)

Show the axon branching into 2–3 terminal boutons that approach the postsynaptic
membrane of a target cell (muscle fiber or second neuron membrane visible as a
horizontal green band along the bottom-right).

- **Axon terminals (boutons)** — rounded, swollen knobs in teal-green at the ends
  of the axon branches. Each bouton is clearly larger than the axon shaft.
- **Synaptic vesicles** — numerous small round lavender-purple circles densely packed
  inside each terminal bouton, clustered toward the presynaptic membrane face.
- **Synaptic cleft** — a narrow pale-blue gap (~20–40 nm, represented as a thin
  visible space) between the presynaptic terminal membrane and the postsynaptic membrane.
- **Postsynaptic membrane** — a distinct dark green horizontal membrane band of the
  target cell, thickened slightly (postsynaptic density) directly opposite each bouton.
- **Receptor proteins** — pink/magenta Y-shaped or cylindrical protein complexes
  embedded in and protruding slightly from the postsynaptic membrane, facing the
  synaptic cleft. Show 4–6 receptor proteins per synaptic zone.

### Style and technical specifications

- **Style:** Biological textbook illustration, clean line art with light color fills,
  white background, professional and accurate anatomical style suitable for college placement Biology.
- **Aspect ratio:** Landscape 4:3
- **Resolution:** 1200 × 900 px
- **Color palette:** Distinct colors per structure — soma (soft pink), nucleus
  (violet-purple), dendrites (cyan-blue), axon hillock (orange), axon (pale yellow),
  myelin (cream/ivory), Schwann cell nucleus (green), nodes of Ranvier (exposed axon
  in pink-red), boutons (teal-green), vesicles (lavender-purple), synaptic cleft
  (pale blue), postsynaptic membrane (dark green), receptor proteins (magenta-pink).
- All structures must have **clear landing zones** — each structure should occupy
  enough space that a 20 px marker dot can be placed on it without overlapping
  neighboring structures.
- The axon runs horizontally through the center-right of the image; the soma and
  dendrites anchor the left third; the synaptic inset fills the right third.
- Do not draw any scale bar, legend, title, figure number, or any other text.
